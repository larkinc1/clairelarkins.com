Nutritional Sciences and Dietetics

Hi! I’m Claire, a graduate student at UC Berkeley. My research interests include designing dietary interventions, exploring the gut-brain axis, and expanding global nutrition. I aim to use my creativity to develop innovative solutions that enhance nutritional care.

Email / LinkedIn

Background

Growing up on a farm in rural Oregon has shaped my perspective on the importance of accessibility for individuals and communities to achieve longevity. Since then, I have learned principles of urban agriculture and continue to practice food cultivation in a community setting. I also believe culinary education can enhance self-efficacy in disease management and promote nutrition education, which can help in the prevention and early detection of diet-related chronic diseases. I volunteer and advocate for organizations that help more people access food and nutrition.

Graduate Research and Projects

Human Nutrition Trial

A dietary intervention designed and implemented to evaluate the impact of a high-protein ovo-lacto vegetarian diet on nitrogen balance.

Molecular Nutrition Manuscript

An experiment that investigated changes in liver metabolism in both fed and fasted states when the PPAR-α gene was impaired.

"Neurometabolism: Gamma-aminobutyric Acid"

A paper exploring the metabolic regulation of the nonprotein amino acid and neurotransmitter GABA and its potential use in future targeted therapies for metabolic disorders.

Dietetic Internship @ UCSF

Clinically focused dietetic training at UCSF Health, emphasizing acute care, management, and specialty inpatient care.

Nutrition Research Capstone Project @ Stanford University
